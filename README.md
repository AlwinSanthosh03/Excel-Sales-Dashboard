# 🏦 Bank Customer Churn Analysis & Retention Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

Customer retention is one of the biggest challenges faced by banking organizations. This project focuses on analyzing customer churn patterns and identifying key factors influencing customers to leave the bank.

An interactive **Excel Dashboard** was created to analyze customer demographics, banking behavior, and churn trends to generate actionable business insights.

The objective of this project is to help the bank understand:
- Why customers are leaving
- Which customer segments have higher churn risk
- What strategies can improve customer retention

---

## 🎯 Business Problem Statement

Banks invest significant resources in acquiring new customers, but retaining existing customers is equally important.

The objective of this analysis is to identify:

- Customer churn rate
- High-risk customer segments
- Relationship between products and customer retention
- Impact of geography, salary, age, credit score, and tenure on churn

# 📈 Key Business Findings

## 1. Customer Churn Rate

- Out of 10,000 customers, **2,037 customers exited the bank**.
- Overall churn rate is approximately **20.4%**.
- Nearly **1 out of every 5 customers** is leaving the bank.

---

## 2. Country-wise Churn Analysis

- Germany shows the highest churn percentage.
- France also has a significant churn rate.
- Spain has comparatively lower churn.

### Insight:
The bank should investigate customer experience, service quality, and product offerings in high-churn regions.

---

## 3. Number of Products vs Churn

- Customers using only **one banking product** show the highest churn.
- Customers using multiple products show better retention.

### Insight:
Cross-selling additional banking products can increase customer loyalty.

---

## 4. Credit Score Impact

- Customers with **Good and Fair credit scores** contribute significantly to churn.
- Excellent credit score customers have lower churn.

### Insight:
Credit score alone does not determine churn; customer engagement strategies are required.

---

## 5. Tenure Analysis

Highest churn observed among:

- Growing Customers (3-5 years)
- Established Customers (6-8 years)

### Insight:
Customers in these stages should receive loyalty benefits and personalized engagement.

---

## 6. Gender-wise Churn

- Female customers contribute approximately **45% of churn cases**
- Male customers contribute approximately **55%**

### Insight:
Customer experience analysis can be performed separately for different customer segments.

---

## 7. Salary Category Analysis

Higher churn is observed among:

- Low Salary customers (<50K)
- Lower Middle Salary customers

### Insight:
Affordable financial products and personalized offers may improve retention.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

✅ Create targeted retention campaigns for high-risk customers.

✅ Improve customer engagement in Germany and other high churn regions.

✅ Encourage customers with one product to adopt additional banking services.

✅ Introduce loyalty programs for customers with 3-8 years tenure.

✅ Provide customized financial benefits based on customer segments.
